Why Vega Matters

The Vega ecosystem — Vega, Vega-Lite, Vega-Altair, VegaFusion, vl-convert — is open-source data visualization infrastructure. Charts render natively in any browser or server-side; Vega-Altair brings the same grammar to Python.

  • 44+ million monthly PyPI downloads for Altair
  • 3+ million monthly CDN downloads for Vega and Vega-Lite
  • One of the most widely used visualization stacks in the Python and web ecosystems
  • Trusted by companies, research institutions, data teams, and tool vendors worldwide
Why Sponsorship Is Needed

Vega is maintained by a small volunteer team. Without sustained funding, roadmap progress slows, maintenance becomes harder, and the ecosystem risks stagnation.

Your sponsorship directly unlocks: faster feature development, improved documentation, reliable long-term maintenance, and a healthier ecosystem for everyone who builds on Vega — including companies, startups, research labs, and data teams.

What Your Sponsorship Funds

We are direct about where the money goes:

  • Core maintainers — Partial compensation for maintainers who currently work on Vega in their spare time. Essential for long-term sustainability.
  • College student contributors — Scoped features under maintainer supervision; valuable experience and real impact on the codebase. Previous support has sponsored core improvements to the development ecosystem
  • Infrastructure & documentation — Hosting, issue triage, and ongoing documentation improvements.
  • Core-developer or user conference — Where roadmap direction is set and contributors meet; attendance funded for core developers. We hosted a Vega conference in 2023a with sponsorship from the Moore foundation.

Active Roadmap

Areas where funding will have immediate impact:

  • Performance — Scaling Vega-Altair to datasets with over 1 million rows, benefiting Python-based data science and scientific communities. GPU-accelerated rendering in Vega is also on the horizon.
  • Agentic support — MCP integration, improved validation errors with actionable suggestions, links to relevant docs, and repository-level agent skills. Making Vega a first-class tool in AI-assisted data workflows.
  • Gridded data & arrays — Native support for images, matrices, rasters via the Array Interchange Protocol.
  • Animation — Declarative animated transitions for all chart types.
  • Language extensions — New statistical chart types, richer geospatial interactions.
  • Ecosystem integration — Deeper ties to PyData, DataFrames, dashboards, and documentation.

Organizations at the Partner tier are invited to share priorities with the steering committee ahead of each planning cycle. Input is heard; it is not binding.
