The Vega ecosystem - Vega, Vega-Lite, Vega-Altair, VegaFusion, vl-convert - is open-source data visualization infrastructure. Vega charts render natively in any browser or server-side; Vega-Altair brings the same grammar to Python. With over 44 million monthly PyPI downloads, it is one of the most widely used visualization stacks in the Python and web ecosystems.

The project is maintained by a small volunteer team. We are seeking organizational sponsors to sustain and extend this infrastructure - and to make the roadmap move faster.

We're grateful to Omni for their support as an early sponsor.

Vega is a NumFOCUS sponsored project. All funds are managed transparently through NumFOCUS (501(c)(3)); contributions may be tax-deductible.

What sponsorship funds

We are direct about what the money goes toward:

Student projects: We fund students to implement specific, scoped features under maintainer supervision. Projects typically run for multiple months, so substantial work gets done; for students, it's valuable experience that can put them in a strong position for future careers.

Bounty system: Sponsors contribute to a shared pool that funds targeted contributions from the broader community - a way to move high-priority issues forward without requiring a full student engagement.

Maintainer compensation: Core maintainers currently work on Vega in their spare time. Sponsorship lets us partially compensate them for their time, which is essential for long-term sustainability.

Annual conference: We plan to organize a Vega core-developer summit. Core developers' attendance is funded; key users and community members are welcome on a self-funded basis. The summit is where roadmap direction gets set and contributors get to meet.

Active roadmap

Areas where funding will have immediate impact:

  • Performance: Scaling Vega-Altair to datasets with over 1 million rows, benefiting Python-based data science and scientific communities. GPU-accelerated rendering in Vega is also on the horizon.
  • Agentic support: MCP integration, improved validation errors with actionable suggestions, links to relevant docs, and repository-level agent skills. Making Vega a first-class tool in AI-assisted data workflows.
  • Gridded data & array support: Vega-Lite currently requires tidy tabular data. We are extending the language to natively support gridded datasets (images, matrices, rasters) by adopting the Array Interchange Protocol.
  • Animation: Declarative animated transitions for all chart types, integrating already-published research.
  • Language extensions: Expanding the core building blocks: statistical chart types not yet possible or convenient to create, and richer geospatial interactions building on altair_tiles.
  • Ecosystem integration: Deeper integration with the PyData and scientific Python communities: DataFrame and dashboard ecosystems, developer experience tooling, and continued documentation development.

Sponsors at the Sponsor tier are invited to share priorities with the steering committee ahead of each planning cycle. Input is heard; it is not binding.
